## 4. Known Logistics Pain Points

### 1. Facility Consolidation = Yard Congestion
The closure of 3 Missouri plants means **remaining facilities absorb more volume**. When you concentrate production into fewer plants, you get:
- More trailers in the yard at each remaining facility
- Longer dock queues
- Greater scheduling complexity
- Higher demurrage costs

**This is happening RIGHT NOW (Oct 2025 announcement, closures phasing through 2026).**

### 2. Product Variety & Multi-Temperature Complexity
General Mills operates across **4 temperature zones** (ambient, frozen, refrigerated, ultra-cold). At a typical multi-category facility:
- Trailers carrying different temperature products compete for the same dock doors
- Dock scheduling requires temperature-zone coordination
- A trailer left sitting in a yard on a hot Atlanta day with Häagen-Dazs inside = quality risk

### 3. Seasonal Demand Spikes
- **Baking season** (Oct–Dec): Pillsbury, Betty Crocker — massive surge
- **Snack season** (summer): Nature Valley, fruit snacks
- **Pet food** (steady but high volume): Blue Buffalo
- **Holiday cereal promotions**: Cheerios, Lucky Charms limited editions
- Seasonal peaks = 30–50% throughput surges at key facilities → yard chaos

### 4. Blue Buffalo Integration Complexity
- Acquired in 2018, still being rationalized (3 pet food plants closing)
- Pet food supply chain runs somewhat separately — integration into unified yard/dock operations is ongoing
- Different carriers, different schedules, different dock requirements

### 5. 3PL Carrier Coordination
- General Mills doesn't own its fleet — relies on contract carriers
- **Driver wait times are a carrier satisfaction issue** — carriers who experience long waits will deprioritize General Mills loads during tight capacity markets
- In a driver-shortage environment, **yard efficiency = carrier relationship management**

### 6. New DC Ramp-Up
- Belvidere DC opened April 2024 — still optimizing operations
- New facilities often run 15–25% below optimal dock utilization in their first 18 months
- Opportunity to implement YardFlow as part of the new DC standard operating procedure
