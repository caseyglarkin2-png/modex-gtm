## 4. Campbell's Logistics Challenges

### Seasonal Demand Complexity
- **Soup season** (Oct–Mar) creates massive demand swings — plants and DCs must absorb 2-3x volume surges
- Snacks are more year-round but spike around holidays, Super Bowl, summer
- Seasonal ramp requires flexible dock scheduling and trailer staging

### Product Mix Complexity
- **Shelf-stable** (canned soup, broth, sauces): high-volume, palletized, heavy
- **Refrigerated** (Rao's fresh sauces, noosa yoghurt): temperature-controlled, time-sensitive
- **Snacks** (Goldfish, pretzels, chips): lightweight but bulky, different handling
- **Baked goods** (Pepperidge Farm): fragile, short shelf-life
- Each category has different dock requirements, trailer types, and turn-time pressures

### Network Consolidation = More Throughput Pressure
- Closing Tualatin and shrinking Jeffersonville means **remaining plants must handle MORE volume**
- Maxton expansion from $150M investment means significantly higher inbound/outbound trailer traffic
- Kettle chip capacity increase = more raw material inbound + more finished goods outbound
- **Every remaining facility becomes a higher-stakes bottleneck**

### Sovos Integration Adds Complexity
- Rao's premium products flowing through Campbell's distribution network
- New SKUs, new suppliers, new co-manufacturer shipments to coordinate
- Premium brand = premium service level expectations (no detention, no damage)

### Driver Detention Exposure
- 24 plants + DCs + co-manufacturers = massive dock footprint
- CPG industry average detention: 1.5–2 hours per stop
- At Campbell's scale (~$9.6B revenue), even 15 minutes saved per dock turn across the network = millions in carrier cost avoidance and goodwill

## 5. Yard/Dock Pain Points (Inferred)

While no public Campbell's-specific yard management complaints were found, the structural signals are strong:

1. **High-volume plants running near capacity** post-consolidation — Maxton alone is getting $150M expansion with 100+ new workers, meaning dramatically more truck traffic
2. **Soup season surge** requires rapid trailer cycling at thermal plants — trailers sitting idle in yards = lost production capacity
3. **Mixed temperature requirements** mean dock assignment errors are costly (putting a reefer load at a dry dock wastes time)
4. **Co-manufacturer coordination** — Campbell's relies heavily on contract manufacturers; inbound from these partners needs tight scheduling
5. **The $230M optimization plan explicitly targets "agile" facilities** — agility at the dock means visibility into what's in your yard and what's coming next
