## 4. Known Logistics Pain Points

### 1. Brand-New CSCO + 30+ Facilities + No Standardized Yard Protocol
Bonifant inherits a manufacturing footprint that grew over **decades** of bolt-on plants and acquisitions (Justin's, Columbus, Planters, Jennie-O, Applegate). Each plant built its own gate process, its own spotter dispatch, its own dock-scheduling tribal knowledge. There is **no public evidence of a network-wide YMS standard** at Hormel today. New leadership at this scale is the rare moment when "we're going to do this one way across all sites" is politically possible.

### 2. USDA / FSIS-Regulated Meat Plants Don't Pause Inbound
Live-hog and live-turkey inbound at protein plants run on biological clocks. **Yard backups translate into production-line consequences within hours**, not days, at sites like Austin MN and Algona IA. Beverage plants can buffer with finished-goods inventory; protein plants cannot.

### 3. Multi-Temperature Trailer Mix at Single Sites
Many Hormel plants ship a mix of ambient (canned SPAM), refrigerated (Black Label bacon, Cure 81 ham), and frozen (Jennie-O) out of the same yard. **Trailers competing for dock doors by temp zone is a daily scheduling problem** that's invisible to a TMS and invisible to a WMS — it lives in the yard.

### 4. Tucker Closure → Volume Concentration at Remaining Bacon Sites
The bacon-line shutdown moves Tucker volume onto a smaller set of sites. **Yard pressure increases at the receiving plants right now**, in the same fiscal year a new CSCO is reading the operations.

### 5. Avian-Flu-Driven Turkey Plant Variability
Jennie-O plants face **uneven raw-material flow** due to HPAI culling. Yard ops at turkey plants have to absorb idle hours, then surge hours. **Variability — not steady-state volume — is the worst kind of yard problem** because schedule-based dock booking degrades fastest.

### 6. New Southaven DC Ramp
A brand-new DC is in **months 8–12 of operation** — exactly the window where dock utilization is still 15–25 points below target as processes settle. Any standard installed now becomes the standard everywhere else.

### 7. Carrier Detention / 3PL Relationship Risk
With a logistics-vendor-partner running Southaven and 3PL carriers handling the bulk of outbound, **carrier wait time at Hormel facilities is a procurement-leverage issue**. Drivers tell their dispatchers which shippers waste their time. In a tight market, that determines who gets capacity.

### 8. T&M's Logistics Pillar Has No Marquee Project (Yet)
T&M's named wins so far: **o9 (planning)**, **Southaven (distribution)**, **Tucker (manufacturing-footprint optimization)**, **CTO + Data & Analytics Office (technology layer)**. There is **no named, marquee project for yard / dock / logistics-execution**. That hole is conspicuous, and it's the kind of hole a new CSCO is going to want to fill in his first 12 months.
