## 3. Current State, Initiatives, and Pain Points

### Tariff overhang ($1.7B net 2025 → $2.6B projected 2026)
Cat publicly disclosed **~$1.8B gross / ~$1.7B net tariff exposure in 2025**, with the 2026 projection rising to **~$2.6B gross**, of which **~$800M lands in Q1 2026 alone**. The tariff line is a top-three margin headwind on every earnings call. *Tariffs on imported components magnify the value of every minute saved at the gate, dock, and yard* — because higher landed cost amplifies the per-unit drag of dwell.

### Record backlog, supply-chain-paced fulfillment
On the Q1 2026 call (Apr 30, 2026), management said growth in Power & Energy is "paced by the timing of capacity expansions and **supply-chain readiness**, with some of the record backlog to be fulfilled over several years." Translation: throughput, not demand, is the binding constraint. **Yard turn-time is throughput.**

### Lafayette $725M engine campus expansion
A 300k-sq-ft addition at the Large Engine Center is underway. New capacity = new yard, new dock count, new component-inbound and finished-engine-outbound flows to design. **Greenfield yard standardization is cheaper than retrofit.** This is the kind of project where a YMS gets specified into the build plan if it gets in early.

### Dealer inventory de-stock cycle
Q4 2025 results flagged that **dealer inventory decreased more in Q4 2025 than Q4 2024** — Cat's volume is currently being suppressed by dealers working down stock. When the cycle inverts (dealers re-stock against the record backlog), parts-depot outbound volume spikes. The 21-depot network has to be ready for the surge **without adding sites**.

### $250M inventory pull from connected-asset visibility
Cat publicly attributes >$250M of inventory reduction to embedded-sensor visibility into in-transit inventory. This is the same playbook YardFlow runs at the yard layer — it is *familiar logic*, not a new sale. The pitch is: "you did this for in-transit; the next $X is inside the gate."

### Scope-3 / sustainability commitments `[unverified specifics]`
Cat reports Scope-1/2 emissions and has stated 2030 sustainability goals; Scope-3 logistics specifics are less clean than General Mills or P&G. Idle-time emissions reductions from yard efficiency are a legitimate but secondary line of argument here.

### CEVA award (May 2025) — relationship signal
CEVA was named **2024–2025 Integrated Logistics Supplier of the Year** for air-freight excellence, presented by Poorman herself. Two implications: (1) air-freight performance is on her measurable scorecard, which means parts-shortage / line-down recovery loops run through her org; (2) she is willing to publicly recognize a 3PL — **she is not anti-vendor**, she is pro-performing-vendor.

## 4. YardFlow Opportunity — Caterpillar

### Why Cat is a different shape from the CPG anchor
The Tier-1 CPG anchor's network (237 facilities, 52 factories, 185 depots, multi-year contract, displaced an incumbent yard system) is a **finished-goods, high-velocity, repeatable-SKU flow**. Cat is the opposite shape: **fewer, larger, lumpier movements**, with two distinct yard surfaces:

| Yard surface | Characteristic | Relevance |
|---|---|---|
| **Parts depot yards** (Morton, Spokane, Waco, Clayton OH, Atlanta, York, ~15 more) | Looks more like CPG: high cadence, dealer-bound mixed loads, dock-door bound | Strongest YardFlow analog to the anchor |
| **Plant yards** (Decatur, East Peoria, Mapleton, Lafayette, Sanford, Clayton NC) | Inbound-component dominant; outbound finished-machine on heavy-haul; lower velocity, higher per-unit value | Different unit economics; per-trailer value is 10–100× CPG |

### The Primo Brands comparable, applied
Primo Brands (the public comparable; not the un-name-able anchor) operates **45 plants** with high-cadence outbound finished-goods. Cat's parts depot tier is cadence-comparable; Cat's plant tier is not. **Lead with parts depots.** Plants come later, after a depot wins and the org gets comfortable.

### The math (parts depot tier)
- **21 depots × ~40 outbound trailers/day average** `[unverified — modeled estimate]` ≈ 840 trailer movements/day
- Cut 24 minutes off each trailer turn → ~336 hours/day saved → **~120,000 hours/year of driver + equipment time** released
- At a heavy-haul-influenced freight cost basis, the per-hour value is meaningfully higher than CPG; demurrage and detention exposure on specialized equipment can run 2–3× the CPG benchmark
- Even before pricing the dollars: every hour of dock capacity recovered is **dealer fill-rate** that doesn't require new capex

### Five reasons Margaret Poorman could care
1. **Tariff-amplified throughput.** When net tariff cost is $1.7B → $2.6B, every minute saved at the dock is worth more in 2026 than it was in 2024. The yard becomes a margin lever, not just an ops lever
2. **Backlog is supply-chain-paced.** Cat told the street the constraint is supply-chain readiness. YardFlow lifts effective dock capacity at zero capex — the cleanest answer to "how do we ship more without building more"
3. **Lafayette and other expansions are greenfield.** Standardize the yard layer before the dock doors are pouring concrete — cheaper than retrofit, and the divisional VP for integrated logistics is the right decision-maker for "do we put a YMS in the build spec"
4. **Helios completion.** Cat's strategic story is unified data through Helios, surfaced through Cat AI Assistant. The yard is a **data hole** in that picture — gate-in / dock-in events for dealer-bound loads aren't in Helios today. YardFlow can feed it
5. **CEVA precedent.** She publicly rewards 3PLs and tech vendors who perform. The award framework signals she will sign and renew with vendors that hit measurable scorecards. **Get measured, win the renewal.**

### Where this is *not* a fit (be honest)
- **Plant yards with heavy-haul finished-goods outbound** are a worse first-deployment than depots — lower trailer cadence, more bespoke routing, more permitting complexity. Don't lead there
- **PINC/Kaleris incumbency** is plausible at one or more Cat sites `[unverified]`. If true, this is a displacement sale, not a greenfield sale, at those sites — different motion
- **Cat's culture rewards internal champions.** A cold pitch from a vendor with no Cat dealer ties is a slower sale than a CPG cold pitch. Plan a 6–9 month courtship, not a 60-day cycle
