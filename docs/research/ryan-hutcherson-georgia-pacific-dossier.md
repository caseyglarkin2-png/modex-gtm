## 4. Known Logistics Pain Points

### 1. The Mill-to-Converter Inbound Problem
GP's pulp and paper mills run 24/7. Converted products (tissue, packaging) come from separate facilities. **The inbound rail-and-truck flow into converter plants is the highest-volume, highest-variability yard problem in the network.** A pulp mill running flat-out and a converter plant with a full yard creates a backup that propagates upstream into mill production economics — much higher stakes than a CPG DC missing an outbound window.

### 2. Building Products Flatbed Yards
Plywood, OSB, and lumber move on flatbeds. Flatbed operations have **fundamentally different yard ergonomics** than dry van: tarping bays, securement areas, mixed equipment types, weather sensitivity. Most YMS products are dry-van-first. **This is a wedge where YardFlow can demonstrate operational understanding that competitors will miss.**

### 3. Anchor Packaging Integration (Active)
Four newly-acquired plants now inside the GP perimeter. Standard playbook: 18–24 months of "do they get the standard stack or run separate?" debates. A YMS conversation today catches Anchor before the Infor decision gets made.

### 4. Retail Customer Scorecard Pressure
GP sells to Walmart, Costco, Lowe's, Home Depot, Sam's Club. **OTIF penalties at these accounts are existential.** Yard delays at the GP outbound DC translate directly into Walmart MABD (Must Arrive By Date) misses → chargebacks → CX reporting up to Ryan. This is the metric that lives on his dashboard.

### 5. KBX as Captive Carrier
KBX is technically Koch family, but it operates with internal P&L discipline. GP plants that consistently waste KBX driver time are **transferring margin from one Koch entity to another** — and the MBM operating model is explicit that this is a knowable, measurable misallocation. **A YardFlow business case that quantifies "Koch-internal margin transfer due to GP-facility dwell" is rhetorically powerful in MBM language.**

### 6. Ocean Export Yards (Cellulose)
GP cellulose ships globally — Switzerland, Uruguay, China references in the Infor announcement. Port-adjacent yards have container-vessel-cutoff pressure that mainland yards don't. **project44 covers visibility but the on-the-ground yard execution at port-adjacent sites is its own problem.**

### 7. Sustainability / Idle Emissions
GP publishes a Corporate Stewardship Report. Diesel idle at facility gates is a Scope 1/3 contribution. Reduced dwell = measurable carbon reduction. Less of a primary driver than at a more sustainability-marketed CPG, but still a credible secondary KPI.

## 5. Yard Management & Dock Scheduling Intelligence

### What We Know (Documented)
GP has **two yard-adjacent technology layers already in production**:

1. **Infor CloudSuite Yard Management** — deployed at 4+ Cellulose mills (Sept 2023) and rolling into Containerboard (5 mills, 70 warehouses, Phase 2)
2. **project44 yard module** — self-service gate kiosks, automated driver check-ins. Documented 10→2 minute entry time, 80% manual-process reduction

This is not a greenfield account. Anyone pitching "you don't have a YMS" will be politely shown the door inside 90 seconds.

### The Real Opening
Both deployed systems are **gate-and-visibility products**, not **execution-and-orchestration products**. The published case-study numbers are about *check-in speed* and *manual labor reduction*, not about *dock-utilization* or *trailer-to-door cycle time inside the yard*.

The question to ask Ryan:

> "After the gate kiosk, what's the average time a trailer spends from check-in to door assignment to door release? And do you have facility-by-facility variance data on it?"

If the answer is "we measure gate, we measure exit, we don't measure the middle" — that is the YardFlow-shaped hole. The 237-facility CPG anchor lived in exactly that hole before YardFlow.

### The Visibility Gap (Refined)
GP has best-in-class tools for:
- Demand planning (AI/ML, Infor, Coleman)
- Manufacturing operations (500K sensors, AWS, ML)
- Transportation visibility (project44 truckload + ocean)
- Gate and kiosk (project44)
- ERP-level yard management (Infor)
- **In-yard execution layer between gate and dock — the 60+ minutes that determines whether Walmart MABD gets hit** — this is where the published numbers go quiet

This is the **invisible-middle-of-yard problem**. Inside that window: trailer hostling, door reassignment, exception management, driver communication, dispatch decisions. Gate kiosks don't fix it. Visibility platforms don't fix it. ERP yard modules describe it but don't orchestrate it.
