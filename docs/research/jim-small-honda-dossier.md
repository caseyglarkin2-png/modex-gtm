## 2. HLNA — Operational Footprint

### What HLNA Actually Is
Honda Logistics North America is the in-house logistics arm for Honda's North American manufacturing footprint. It is **not** a 3PL that Honda hires — it **is** Honda. Headquartered in East Liberty, OH, HLNA is structured as a holding company over a set of operating subsidiaries that run distinct geographies and service mixes:

| Subsidiary | Role | Geography |
|---|---|---|
| **Midwest Express, Inc. (MEI)** | Lead logistics provider — line-side delivery, sequenced parts, milkrun coordination | East Liberty OH, Greensburg IN, Lincoln AL, Timmonsville SC |
| **New South Express (NSE)** | Logistics services for Honda's Alabama footprint | Lincoln, AL and Talladega, AL (operational since 2000) |
| **South East Express (SEE)** | Acquired into HLNA — South-region operations | Southeast US |
| **One Solution Logistics of Indiana (OSL)** | Indiana plant logistics | Greensburg IN |
| **Komyo America (KMA)** | Komyo Logistics affiliate — global Honda parts logistics support | Multi-region |

This is a deliberately federated structure. Each subsidiary runs its own roster, its own carrier relationships, its own yard. HLNA at the holding level owns the standards, the technology choices, and the executive promotions.

### Manufacturing Customers HLNA Serves
HLNA is the in-plant inbound parts logistics layer (and in many cases the finished-vehicle staging layer) for:

| Performance Manufacturing Center (PMC) | Marysville, OH | Acura NSX (limited), specialty hand-built lines | Low-volume |
| Anna Engine Plant (AEP) | Anna, OH | Engines + EV battery packs (post-2025 retool) | Engine + IPU |

## 4. The Yard Pain — Where YardFlow Lands

The published evidence and industry shape combine into a clean picture of where the yard breaks at HLNA:

### Pain 1: Sequencing Cascades At Crossdocks
The East Liberty crossdock processes ~130 inbound trucks daily and 6,000 delivery scans. Inbound parts get sorted from milkrun consolidation into **line-side delivery sequence** — the truck-to-dock-to-line handoff is where JIS (Just-In-Sequence) is either preserved or destroyed. A truck waiting 20 extra minutes at the gate doesn't just delay one trailer; it disrupts the sequence that the entire crossdock day was choreographed around. **YardFlow turns the gate-to-dock leg from minutes-of-uncertainty into minutes-of-known-state.**

### Pain 2: Drop-And-Hook Discipline At Scale
The 2015 *Automotive Logistics* reporting explicitly noted that Honda's plants use "drop and hook" trailer procedures specifically to minimize driver wait time, in response to the labor constraints of the regional carrier network. **This is the right answer to the wrong granularity** — drop-and-hook works only when the yard can find the dropped trailer instantly, route the hostler to the right slot, and synchronize the hook to dock availability. Without yard visibility, drop-and-hook becomes a yard-jockey scavenger hunt. YardFlow operationalizes drop-and-hook at the trailer level.

### Pain 3: New EV Inbound Patterns From Day One
The Ohio EV Hub retool is *replacing* the inbound supplier base in real time. ICE-era engine subassemblies coming inbound to MAP/ELP are being phased out; battery packs from Anna (sourced from LGES Jeffersonville) and new EV-specific parts from new Tier-1 suppliers are coming in. **The 2026–2027 yard at Marysville will not look like the 2024 yard.** Honda has publicly committed to a "Simple Logistics" redesign — yard execution is part of that surface, whether they've named it yet or not.

### Pain 4: Federated Subsidiary Structure
HLNA's MEI/NSE/SEE/OSL subsidiary structure means each plant region has its own ops culture and its own systems. There is **no single yard management standard** across HLNA today, as far as public sources show `[unverified]`. This is a feature, not a bug, for YardFlow: the holding company can pilot at one subsidiary's geography, prove it, and roll the standard across the federation. **It's a textbook land-and-expand path.**

### Pain 5: Outbound FVL / Rail Yard Coordination
With 82% of Honda vehicles moving outbound by rail, the **finished-vehicle yard at Marysville/East Liberty/Lincoln/Greensburg** is its own complex orchestration problem — auto-haulers and rail loadouts share the staging space, and the inbound parts yard is feet away. Finished-vehicle yard pain is adjacent to inbound parts yard pain, often coordinated by separate teams. The rare YMS that handles both is a force multiplier inside an OEM.

### Pain 6: Labor Math
With 3.5% unemployment around Marysville/East Liberty, **the constraint is people, not capital.** Every dock door that can be served by the same yard staff after a YardFlow rollout is a marginal vehicle that can be built without hiring someone Honda can't find. This is the math that closes the deal.
