### JIT/JIS Delivery Model — The Heartbeat of Ford's Supply Chain
Ford pioneered modern assembly line manufacturing and runs one of the most sophisticated **Just-in-Time (JIT) / Just-in-Sequence (JIS)** systems in the world:

- **Inbound parts:** Delivered in precise sequence to the assembly line, often with **<2-hour buffer windows**
- **Sequenced parts (JIS):** Seats, dashboards, axles delivered in exact build-order sequence — a wrong sequence = line stoppage
- **Milk runs:** Ford's logistics providers run multi-stop pickup routes from Tier 1/2 suppliers, consolidating loads
- **Cross-docks:** Parts flow through regional cross-dock facilities before final delivery to plant
- **Line-side delivery:** Trucks dock and parts go directly to the line — no warehouse staging
- **Finished vehicle:** Completed units drive off the line into **massive finished vehicle yards** (often 5,000-15,000 units) awaiting rail/truck transport to dealers

### Finished Vehicle Logistics (FVL)
- Ford uses dedicated **vehicle processing centers (VPCs)** — finished vehicles are stored, inspected, accessorized, and shipped
- Major rail yards at or near plants (CSX, Norfolk Southern, BNSF, UP)
- **Truck carriers** haul ~60% of finished vehicles; rail handles ~40%
- Average finished vehicle sits in yard/transit for **40-60 days** from production to dealer delivery
- **Vehicle yard management** is a known pain point — tracking 10,000+ vehicles across acres of lot space

## 4. Known Logistics Pain Points — YardFlow Relevance

### 🔴 CRITICAL: Inbound Parts Yard Management
- **Problem:** Ford plants receive 300-800 inbound trailers per day at major assembly plants (Dearborn, Kentucky Truck, Kansas City)
- **JIT precision:** Parts must arrive within ±30 minute windows. A late trailer = potential **line-down event** costing **$20,000-50,000+ per minute**
- **Yard congestion:** Trailers queue at gates, wait for dock assignment, compete for staging spots
- **Driver detention:** OTR drivers report **2-4 hour average wait times** at Ford plants (trucker forums, DAT/Convoy data)
- **YardFlow fit:** Real-time trailer tracking, automated dock scheduling, priority queuing for JIS loads

### 🔴 CRITICAL: Finished Vehicle Yard Management
- **Problem:** Ford's finished vehicle yards hold 5,000-15,000+ vehicles per plant
- **Tracking challenge:** Which vehicles are ready for shipment? Which need holds (quality, accessory, recall)?
- **Carrier coordination:** Rail and truck carriers need precise bay assignments and load sequencing
- **VPC throughput:** Vehicle processing centers are bottlenecks — wash, inspect, accessorize, load
- **Dwell time:** Vehicles sitting in yard = working capital locked up. Ford finances ~4.4M vehicles/year in inventory
- **YardFlow fit:** Vehicle yard location tracking, carrier scheduling, dwell time analytics, hold management

### 🟡 HIGH: Supplier Coordination & Gate Management
- **Problem:** 1,200+ production suppliers shipping to 25+ plants across NA
- **Gate throughput:** Commercial vehicle gates at major plants handle 500+ check-ins/check-outs daily
- **Appointment compliance:** Suppliers with poor on-time arrival disrupt JIS sequencing
- **YardFlow fit:** Appointment scheduling, gate automation, supplier scorecard data

### 🟡 HIGH: Multi-Modal Yard Complexity
- Ford yards manage simultaneously: **inbound parts trailers, outbound finished vehicles, rail cars, employee parking, supplier shuttles, prototype/engineering vehicles**
- Major plants like Dearborn Truck have yards spanning **hundreds of acres**

## 5. Automotive Yard Management — Why It's HUGE

- **Automotive YMS market** is one of the largest segments of yard management (~35% of total YMS market)
- Every major automaker (Toyota, GM, Stellantis, BMW, Mercedes) either has a legacy YMS or is actively looking to upgrade
- **Key drivers:**
  - JIT/JIS demands make dock scheduling precision mission-critical
  - Finished vehicle logistics (FVL) requires tracking thousands of high-value assets
  - Union labor rules complicate yard operations (UAW at Ford)
