## 4. The 3PL Adjustment — Why Kenco Is a Different Kind of Target

### YardFlow's 3PL Thesis (vs. End-Shipper Pitch)
For end-shippers like General Mills, YardFlow is a tool they buy and operate at their own facilities. For a 3PL like Kenco, YardFlow is something fundamentally different:

> **A capability they buy once and resell as a managed-service offering to their 390+ shipper customers.**

Kenco's business model already does this with WMS (Blue Yonder), TMS, AMRs (GreyOrange/Locus), and labor management. They embed third-party tech, wrap it in operational service, and bill it through to the shipper. **YardFlow fits this exact distribution model** — but for the layer of the warehouse that today is mostly clipboard-and-radio.

### Why This Is a Partnership Conversation, Not a Vendor Sale
A standard YMS pitch says: *"Replace your spreadsheet with our software."* That's a procurement conversation.

The Kenco pitch needs to say: *"Embed our yard-execution layer into your distribution-technology product, the same way you've done with GreyMatter. We bring a software stack and pricing model designed for multi-tenant 3PL deployment. You bring 141 facilities and the customer relationships. We split the value."*

This reframes Kristi's evaluation:
- Not: *"Do I want to buy this?"* (procurement instinct: defer)
- Instead: *"Is this a product I can put in my catalog and sell to my customers?"* (innovation/transformation instinct: lean in)

### Kristi Specifically Is the Right Door
Other 3PL contacts (operations VPs, IT VPs, sales VPs) would route this to vendor procurement. Kristi's entire job — old role and new role — is **partnership-vetting and customer-lifecycle transformation.** Her published methodology:
1. Identify a customer-side pain point
2. Pilot a partner technology in the Innovation Lab
3. Roll out at one customer site
4. Productize and scale across the customer base

**This is exactly the path YardFlow needs to walk.** GreyOrange is the most recent precedent.

## 6. Competitive Intelligence

### What Kenco Already Has
- **WMS:** Blue Yonder (confirmed customer; productivity gains case study published)
- **YMS:** Blue Yonder + PINC (both deployed for specific customers, per Armstrong & Associates 3PL profile)
- **AMR / Orchestration:** Locus Robotics + GreyOrange GreyMatter (5-year strategic partnership, 20→50 site rollout)
- **TMS:** Internal Kenco Transportation Management entity, plus integration with shipper-side TMS via Boomi (named Boomi customer)
- **Slotting:** SLOT DC
- **Telematics:** Kenco FleetCloud
- **Dock displays:** Rocket Turn (pilot/customer-deployment)
- **Documentation:** LoadProof

### Where YardFlow Wins
- **Multi-tenant by design** — PINC and Blue Yonder YMS are anchored to single-customer deployments. A 3PL multi-client warehouse with 4 shippers sharing a yard is the worst-case scenario for those tools. YardFlow's data model is multi-tenant from the foundation
- **Execution layer, not records layer** — PINC/Blue Yonder are systems of record. YardFlow is a system of execution: real-time gate-to-dock orchestration, driver-facing workflows, dock-scheduling integration
- **Embeddable / white-label-ready** — designed to be resold as a Kenco-branded capability
- **Faster pilot footprint** — single-site, 90-day pilot is the natural unit of work, matching the Innovation Lab's published cadence
- **Complements GreyMatter** — GreyMatter orchestrates the inside of the building; YardFlow orchestrates everything outside the dock door. The data handoff between the two is the real product story

### Risks & Watch-outs
- **Kristi will know the YMS landscape cold.** Do not oversell category novelty. Sell category *adjacency*.
- **Pritzker ownership** means Kenco is being run for growth and margin — innovation pitches need a clear path to billable customer revenue, not just internal cost savings
- **The Innovation Lab is a funnel, not a finish line.** Many startups pilot at Kenco and never scale. The pitch needs to make the path from pilot to commercial deployment explicit upfront
- **Long sales cycles for 3PL embeds** — even with Kristi as champion, productizing for 390+ customers is a multi-quarter effort. Set expectations accordingly.

## 7. Why Kenco Matters Strategically to YardFlow

Most YardFlow targets are end-shippers — one logo, one yard footprint, one buying decision. Kenco is structurally different:

- **141 facilities** under one roof of decision-making
- **390+ shipper customers** that Kenco can in turn introduce to YardFlow
- **A standing innovation function** with a published methodology that YardFlow's pilot model maps onto cleanly

If Kenco productizes YardFlow into its distribution-technology catalog, the unit economics of the partnership likely exceed any single end-shipper deal in YardFlow's pipeline. This is a 1-to-many distribution play disguised as a 1-to-1 sales conversation.

The question is not whether Kenco needs YardFlow internally — they have functioning YMS deployments and competent operations. The question is whether **Kenco's customers** would buy a Kenco-branded yard-execution managed service, and whether YardFlow is the right partner to build it with.

That's the conversation to have with Kristi.
