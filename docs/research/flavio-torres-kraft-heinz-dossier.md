## 5. Known Logistics Pain Points

### 1. Site-Level YMS Doesn't Ladder Up
Twelve sites × six campuses × ~20 years of Kaleris means **a dozen mature site-level yard operations** that nonetheless do not behave as a network. Each site optimizes its own gate, its own dock priority, its own appointment-versus-walk-in mix. Standardization across sites — what Torres did at AB InBev for breweries — has not been done at Kraft Heinz for yards.

### 2. Leaner Inventory = Less Yard Forgiveness
Agile@Scale cut inventory ~20%. Lean networks punish yard variance disproportionately: a 90-minute trailer delay used to land inside safety stock; in the new state it shows up at the shelf. Torres owns the inventory line and the yard, so this pain rolls to the same desk.

### 3. The DeKalb Greenfield
A 775,000 sq ft automated DC will, by 2027, become the single highest-throughput node in the U.S. network. Daifuku-driven automation inside the building demands consistent trailer presentation. **A new DC with no entrenched yard-ops process is the highest-leverage single deployment site in the entire portfolio.**

### 4. Foodservice + Grocery Cadence Mismatch
DeKalb will run >60% of foodservice volume and ~30% of dry grocery. These channels have different appointment patterns, different carrier mixes, different cube/weight profiles. A single site running two cadence patterns is exactly where network-aware yard logic earns its keep.

### 5. Multi-Temp at Most Sites
Ambient + refrigerated + frozen at the same plant means trailers compete for dock doors with different temperature-zone constraints. Site-level Kaleris solves this *at the site*; it does not arbitrate across sites.

### 6. Carrier Relationship Pressure
Kraft Heinz is 3PL/contract-carrier dependent. In a tight freight market, carriers route drivers to shippers who don't burn driver hours. The 50% truck/driver figure from the Stockton case is a 2006 number; carriers' expectations have moved since.

### 7. Manufacturing Modernization Downstream Effects
The $3B plant investment will increase throughput at ~30 plants. Throughput-out-the-door becomes trailer-into-the-yard. Modernizing the building without modernizing the yard creates a known choke point.
