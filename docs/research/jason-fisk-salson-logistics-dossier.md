## 4. The 3PL Adjustment — Why SalSon Is a Different Kind of Target

### YardFlow's 3PL Thesis Applied to SalSon
For end-shippers, YardFlow is a tool deployed at their own yard. For a 3PL like SalSon, YardFlow can be something fundamentally different:

> **A capability SalSon buys once and resells as a branded execution layer to its shipper customers — alongside drayage, transload, and dedicated fleet.**

SalSon already does this distribution model with PINC (records), TMW (dispatch), and the My SalSon portal (visibility). What it does **not** yet have is a packaged execution layer it can sell with its drayage service. That is precisely YardFlow's shape.

### Why This Is a Partnership / Co-Development Conversation
Standard YMS pitch to a 3PL: *"Rip and replace PINC."* That doesn't work — PINC is operationally embedded in Newark and Kaleris is a strategic relationship.

The SalSon pitch needs to say: *"Layer YardFlow's execution surface on top of your existing PINC yard. Wrap it in the My SalSon portal. Sell it to your shipper customers as 'SalSon Yard Visibility' — a differentiated service the other NY/NJ drayage carriers can't match."*

This reframes Fisk's evaluation:
- **Not:** *"Should we change our YMS?"* (answer: no, PINC is fine)
- **Instead:** *"Can we add a margin-accretive, customer-stickiness product to the SalSon service bundle without disrupting the operating layer?"* (PE-CEO answer: lean in)

### Fisk-Specific Hooks (vs. an Operations-VP Pitch)
Fisk's job is **footprint, M&A multiple, and revenue mix.** The YardFlow story for him is:
1. **Differentiated wedge for new shipper wins** — gives the sales team a feature drayage competitors don't have
2. **Pre-positioning for M&A integration** — tuck-in acquisitions arrive with mismatched yard systems; YardFlow becomes the standard execution layer across newly-acquired yards. This is a real PE pain point in roll-ups
3. **Margin attachment** — a sellable SaaS-wrapped service raises EBITDA and re-rates the multiple on exit
4. **Customer retention** — embedded yard visibility creates switching cost for shippers, hardening drayage revenue

## 6. Competitive Intelligence

### What SalSon Already Has
- **YMS:** PINC (now Kaleris) — Newark deployment confirmed; other sites unverified
- **TMS:** TMW (Trimble) — fleet dispatch, route optimization
- **Customer portal:** My SalSon (proprietary)
- **Telematics / tracking:** Container-level tracking via PINC integration
- **Sustainability:** SmartWay; 150+ zero-emission units
- **Fleet:** 1,000+ tractors, ~1,200 trailers, ~2,500 chassis

### Where YardFlow Wins (Against PINC / Kaleris)
- **Execution layer vs. record layer** — Kaleris/PINC tracks what's in the yard; YardFlow orchestrates what happens next (dock-door assignment, driver routing, gate flow). The two are complementary, not competing
- **Multi-tenant by design** — SalSon's seven-company merger is the textbook case for a yard tool with native multi-tenant + multi-site data model. PINC is single-site-anchored in most deployments
- **Shipper-facing surface** — YardFlow's visibility surface can plug into the My SalSon portal without ripping PINC out. This is the partnership architecture
- **Pilot-friendly footprint** — single yard, 90 days, success metrics tied to shipper-experience NPS or specific KPIs (driver wait time at gate, dock-door turn time)

### Risks & Watch-outs
- **PINC is publicly cited by SalSon** — they've put their name to that vendor. Any pitch that even smells like "rip and replace" will get bounced
- **Kaleris is a serious incumbent with M&A-fueled platform breadth** — they will fight to add their own execution layer. Move quickly
- **Fisk's calendar is M&A-heavy** — competing with bolt-on diligence work for his attention is real. The outreach has to be sharp and short
- **The Berritto family is still in the building** — Anthony as Senior Business Advisor, Michael as EVP Sales. They will have opinions on a long-trusted PINC relationship being augmented. Pitch needs to respect, not threaten
- **SalSon is on the M&A path — either acquiring or being acquired eventually** — long sales cycles risk the rug being pulled. A 90-day-to-value pilot is the right pace
