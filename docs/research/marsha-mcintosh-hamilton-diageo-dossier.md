## 4. Known Logistics Pain Points

### 1. Network Rationalization → Volume Concentration
Amherstburg closes. Stitzel-Weller bottling closes. Lebanon throttled. **Volume is concentrating into Plainfield, Shelbyville, Valleyfield, and the brand-new Montgomery plant.**
- Plainfield is already Diageo's largest NA site at 600+ people across the campus
- Now it's absorbing US-bound Crown Royal bottling volume on top of Smirnoff and RTDs
- More trailers, more dock contention, more scheduling complexity at the same campus

### 2. RTD Boom Creates Different Trailer Profile
The RTD category (Smirnoff seltzers, spirits-based canned cocktails) ships in fundamentally different patterns than aged spirits:
- Higher volume, lower margin, faster cycle
- Convenience-channel and grocery-channel destinations (different from the on-premise/spirits route)
- Seasonal summer peaks
- Plainfield Lincolnway can run 25M+ cases/year on RTDs alone — that's a lot of trucks

### 3. Three-Tier Distribution Wholesaler Service Levels
Wholesaler-bound shipments (Southern Glazer's, Republic National, etc.) have **strict appointment windows and OTIF expectations.** Late or out-of-window means chargebacks and de-prioritization. Yard delays directly translate to wholesaler relationship friction.

### 4. Bonded Warehouse and Excise-Tax Complexity
Spirits inventory in TTB-bonded warehouses is tracked at the case/proof-gallon level for federal excise tax. Movement on the yard is a *taxable event* lifecycle:
- Trailer movement between bonded and non-bonded zones must be auditable
- Driver dwell at gate while paperwork resolves is real (and ugly)
- This is **regulated yard movement**, not just operational yard movement — a class of complexity General Mills, Hormel, etc. don't have

### 5. Tariff-Driven Lane Reshuffling
$200M/year in tariff exposure means **the network is actively being re-optimized in 2026.** Lane mix between Canada / US / Mexico facilities is in flux. Yard utilization at any single site is changing month-to-month.

### 6. Aged-Inventory Glut → Throughput, Not Production
With $22B of aged spirits sitting across the industry and 43% inventory-to-revenue at Diageo, the bottleneck has shifted **from "make more" to "move what we have efficiently."** This makes yard/dock throughput the binding constraint, not distillation capacity.

### 7. Brand-New Montgomery Plant in Ramp-Up
The Montgomery facility opened **less than three weeks ago** (April 21, 2026). New plants run at 15–25% below optimal dock utilization for the first 12–18 months. The yard SOP is being written right now. This is the cleanest possible greenfield deployment opportunity in CPG.
