## 5. Known Logistics Pain Points — The YardFlow Opportunity

### 5a. Volume Concentration Into Internal Breweries (95% Internal)
Boston Beer just shifted **10 points of volume from contract brewers to its own breweries** in a single year. The internal yards are absorbing the shift. Margin attribution in Q1 2026 was explicitly **brewery efficiency**. Every percentage point of internal volume going forward depends on **dock and yard throughput at PA, Cincinnati, and Milton DE keeping pace.** This is exactly the binding constraint YardFlow solves.

### 5b. SKU Complexity Mix Shift (Peak-Truly → Diversified)
The yard SOPs from the peak-Truly era (2020–2021) were designed for:
- High-velocity 12-pack canned hard seltzer
- Limited SKU count, predictable wholesaler waves
- Heavy reliance on contract brewers for surge capacity

The 2026 mix is fundamentally different:
- **Twisted Tea** as durable cash cow (different package mix, different on-premise/off-premise split)
- **Sun Cruiser** as spirits-RTD growth (different TTB classification, different wholesaler relationships)
- **Hard MTN Dew** through traditional beer wholesalers (Pepsi-bottler friction adds appointment complexity)
- **Sam Adams + Angry Orchard + Dogfish Head** craft tail (heterogeneous package mix; glass and can)
- **Truly** still meaningful but shrinking

The yard at each internal brewery is now running **2-3x the SKU/wholesaler combination complexity** with the **same dock infrastructure**. This is where dwell time silently expands.

### 5c. Cincinnati's $85M Canning Build Means Cincinnati Is The Pinch Point
The 2020 Cincinnati investment quadrupled canning capacity for Sam Adams, Angry Orchard, Twisted Tea, and Truly. That means Cincinnati is the **packaging-density anchor** for the network. Every quart of canned product that hits Cincinnati needs to leave Cincinnati on a trailer. Volume that used to flow through contract canners now compresses into Cincinnati dock doors. **Cincinnati is the highest-leverage yard in the network for throughput gains.**

### 5d. PA Brewery Is The Largest — And Savastano's Home Base
The PA brewery is the largest in the network. Savastano ran it for 18 months and was promoted out of it. He **knows the yard there personally** — every dock, every spotter, every chronic queue. A pitch about *the PA yard specifically* (not "your yard network") is the only credible opening with him. He will smell generic instantly.

### 5e. The Hard MTN Dew Wholesaler Friction Problem
Q1 2026 earnings called out *"distribution friction with independent Pepsi bottlers"* for Hard MTN Dew. After the February 2024 shift away from Blue Cloud Distribution, Hard MTN Dew now goes through traditional beer wholesalers — but **not** through the same wholesalers as the rest of the Boston Beer portfolio in every market. This means:
- Different wholesaler appointment windows for Hard MTN Dew vs Sam Adams in the same market
- Trailer routing decisions at the brewery yard are more complex
- More risk of "trailer for distributor A is in dock 2, but distributor B's appointment opens at 9am and we don't have a free door"
- Yard orchestration matters more, not less, after the Blue Cloud migration

### 5f. Cold Chain Considerations for Hard Seltzer / Spirits RTDs
Hard seltzer **yeast stability and flavor stability** are temperature-sensitive. Trailers parked in the summer sun at PA or Cincinnati create quality risk if dwell exceeds nominal windows. This is not a generic-CPG concern — it's specifically a **beverage manufacturing** concern. Yard dwell management has a **quality dimension** at Boston Beer that wouldn't appear in a Hormel or General Mills pitch.

### 5g. Aluminum Can Supply Chain
The 2020 Cincinnati canning expansion ties Boston Beer to aluminum can inbound flow. **Aluminum can manufacturers (Ball, Crown, Ardagh) deliver in tightly-scheduled rail and truck waves.** Inbound yard congestion at canning plants is a real, recurring brewery problem. The PINC RFID baseline doesn't optimize inbound; it observes inbound. YardFlow's orchestration does.

### 5h. Glass Bottle Supply Chain
Sam Adams legacy SKUs and craft tail (Dogfish Head, Angry Orchard cider) still run **glass-bottled** at the PA brewery. Glass inbound trailers are heavier per trailer than aluminum can inbound — fewer SKU units per trailer, more trailers per unit, more yard moves. Mixed glass/aluminum inbound is one of the more chaotic patterns in beverage brewery yards.
