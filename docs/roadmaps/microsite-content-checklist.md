# Microsite Content Checklist

Use this checklist before promoting an account microsite to flagship status.

## Narrative Quality

1. Hero states an account-specific thesis, not a generic yard-management claim.
2. Problem framing names a real operating constraint, timing signal, or network pressure.
3. Stakes translate operations friction into executive consequences.
4. CTA asks for a specific next step in the account's language.

## Proof Quality

1. Proof section contains at least one measurable deployment result.
2. Proof ties to the account's vertical and network shape.
3. ROI lines are conservative and explainable.
4. Testimonial or operator proof does not read like placeholder copy.

## Personalization Quality

1. At least three person variants exist for flagship accounts.
2. Variants shift the framing, not just the greeting.
3. Variant framing cites actual named-person evidence when available: mandate, quote, prior role, public language, or known pain point.
4. KPI language matches the individual and their documented operating lens, not just the title.
5. Warm-intro-only accounts do not use cold-booking CTA language.
6. Any role-based hypothesis is clearly a fallback and should be replaced by research before flagship promotion.

## Research Grounding

1. Every flagship variant can point to at least two documented person-specific anchors.
2. Public quotes and known-for language are reflected in copy where useful.
3. Prior-company context is used only when it sharpens the actual thesis for that person.
4. Generic claims that could fit any VP at any CPG account are treated as quality failures.

## Experience Quality

1. Every section has a stable `sectionId`.
2. Every flagship page has a clear proof sequence.
3. Every flagship page builds cleanly and passes route smoke coverage.
4. Analytics instrumentation is present before rollout.
