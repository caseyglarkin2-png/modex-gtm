## Tracking notes — first 48 hours

Watch the following signals across the 10-account batch to validate whether the per-account investment (custom audio brief, coverage-map artifact, hand-shaped framing) is producing differential engagement vs. the generic-microsite baseline:

- **Open / first-touch rate** — personalized URL hits vs. public URL hits within 6h, 24h, 48h windows. The `?p=…` query string is the signal that the named POC opened the link rather than an unrelated viewer.
- **Click-through to /roi** — the ROI calculator is the strongest intent signal short of a meeting request. Track which accounts route there and how long they spend.
- **Scroll depth** — section-by-section depth into the memo. Specifically watch the comparable (Primo) section and the methodology section: those are the two surfaces that disqualify a vendor if the prospect doesn't read them, and qualify the meeting if they do.
- **Audio brief plays** — count audio starts, chapter completions, and replay events. This is the direct test of whether the custom-audio investment pays back: if a meaningful share of openers play the brief past chapter II, the audio is earning its cost. If audio plays are near zero across the batch, the per-account audio investment is not the lever.
- **Return visits** — multi-session opens (e.g., POC opens at 9 AM, returns at 4 PM, returns next morning). Return visits inside 48h are the strongest leading indicator of internal forwarding and meeting-likelihood.
- **Identified company traffic** — Clearbit/RB2B-style enrichment for the public URL: if colleagues at the same company hit the public URL within 48h of the POC opening the personalized URL, the memo is being forwarded internally. That's the highest-value signal in the batch.
- **Reply / meeting rate** — direct replies to any send-channel email, and meeting bookings tied to these slugs. The batch is small enough (n=10) that even one or two qualified meetings constitutes signal worth scaling on; zero meetings across 10 carefully shaped memos is a negative signal worth respecting.
- **Sentiment in any reply text** — pushback on specific claims (the caveat sections invite this explicitly) is a strong qualifying signal; thank-you-not-now replies are weaker but still ordered above silence.

Decision gate for the next batch (proposed): if ≥2 of the 10 accounts produce meeting requests or substantive replies inside 7 days, and audio-play rate exceeds 25% of opens across the batch, scale to a 20-account batch with the same custom-audio treatment. Below either threshold, drop the custom audio from the standard treatment and test a leaner per-account investment on the next 10.
